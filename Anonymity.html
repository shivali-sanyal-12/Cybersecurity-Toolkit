Anonymity & Privacy Techniques

What is Anonymity?

Anonymity is the state of being unidentifiable within a set of subjects, allowing you to browse, communicate, or operate without revealing your real identity or location.

  • Protects user privacy and freedom.
  • Common in whistleblowing, journalism, privacy-conscious browsing.
  • Techniques include using proxy servers, VPNs, Tor network, and anonymous communication tools.

Tor Network Overview

Tor (The Onion Router) enables anonymous communication by routing your traffic through multiple encrypted nodes, obscuring your origin IP address and preventing tracking.

How Tor Works:

  • Your data is encrypted in multiple layers (like an onion).
  • Traffic passes through at least 3 Tor nodes before reaching the destination.
  • Each node peels off a layer of encryption, never knowing both origin and destination.
  • This provides strong anonymity but can reduce browsing speed.
